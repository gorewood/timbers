# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is developers and maintainers working with AI-assisted codebases who need durable reasoning after the original session has disappeared. A secondary audience is engineering leads reading generated reports, release notes, and project history without needing to understand Timbers' storage mechanics first.

## Product Purpose

Timbers captures the what, why, and how of development work as compact, Git-native records, then turns those records into useful project artifacts. The site succeeds when a visitor understands that distinction quickly, trusts the approach, and can inspect real output before installing the CLI.

## Positioning

Git records the change; Timbers preserves the reasoning that makes the change understandable.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: install Timbers and follow the tutorial.
- Secondary CTA: inspect real artifacts generated from Timbers' own ledger.
- The line to remember: "Git knows what changed. Timbers captures why."
- Belief ladder: reasoning disappears; transcripts are too heavy; compact capture is durable; Git-native storage keeps ownership local; real reports prove the ledger remains useful.
- Proof on hand: Timbers' own committed ledger, generated reports, release notes, decision digests, and development history published by this site.

## Brand Personality

Mechanical, material, alive. Timbers should feel like a contemporary working mill: precise enough to trust, tactile enough to remember, and active without becoming noisy. The emotional goal is confident curiosity followed by practical clarity.

## Anti-references

Do not make Timbers look rustic, outdoorsy, or nostalgic. Avoid generic forest photography, fake wood textures, lumberjack motifs, terminal cosplay, beige editorial minimalism, interchangeable SaaS cards, decorative technical diagrams, and motion applied to every section.

## Design Principles

1. Show transformation: raw commits become graded reasoning and useful artifacts.
2. Use real output as proof rather than making abstract product claims.
3. Let brand surfaces be expressive while report surfaces remain quiet and readable.
4. Treat imagery and motion as meaning-bearing parts of the narrative.
5. Keep the publishing output static, host-neutral, fast, and locally owned.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA contrast and keyboard requirements. Preserve visible focus states, semantic structure, usable touch targets, legible long-form typography, reduced-motion alternatives, and complete access to content when animation or custom fonts do not load.
